2017 Winter Break

Photography & Editing

This past winter break I had the chance to travel to Iceland, Paris, and Bordeaux, for a perfect mix of friends, family, nature, and culture. When I found myself with massive amounts of images of stunning Icelandic landscape or shots of quirky museum goers to process every day, I decided to try my hand at Lightroom as an alternative to Adobe Photoshop. While I wouldn't call myself a pro now by any means, I think my editing did improve after three weeks of messing around with the software. The beautiful landscape definitely did most of the heavy lifting. I've come to appreciate Lightroom's speed and accuracy almost as much as the Paris metro or Icelandic skyr. Take a look at some of my favorite snaps. All photos taken with my mom's Canon EOS Rebel T6 (thanks mom, you're the best), and edited in Lightroom 2015.
