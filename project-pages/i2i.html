Ideas to Impact

Graphic Design

From the 2017 spring through the summer I developed graphics and building a curriculum for USC’s first student-led class. From Ideas to Impact is a 2-unit project-based course that helps students through the process of having a measured, mindful, and effective impact at USC. The curriculum is heavily based on design-thinking methodologies from IDEO, d.school, and +Acumen. I’ve developped a brand identity for the class, with an accompanying brand book and digital marketing materials.



The Brand

Logo Iteration

The graphics team and I used the cicada and its three stages of growth to convey the process of taking an idea to fruition. The final logo (right column, bottom) appears in its long, normal, and large logotype form.

Brand Book

In addition to the logo, I put together a brand book to document the class mission and how it should be conveyed with the brand. I used the Spotify Brand Guidelines as a point of reference.

Marketing Flyers

This flyer and its variants was created to reach out to students on campus to join the class.

Takeaways

Maintaining a brand is more difficult than building it

While creating a brand from scratch can be a grueling and creatively taxing undertaking, I learned that maintaining a brand is to a marathon as creating a brand is to a sprint. Brand guidelines play a small part in ensuring that the original assets are used approrpriately by team members for marketing materials, or by external organizations for press and social media posts.

Be clear about what not to do

Emphasizing how to not use a brand is just as important as setting rules for how to use assets. Setting up explicit rules for best practices when using a logo is great, but outlining common cases of misuse could be more useful for the audience that will be using it down the road.
