Batlló

Fashion & Product Design

For this Iovine and Young materials studies project, I wanted to learn more about textiles and was inspired by Gaudi’s architecture. My final product was a handsewn shirt with a pop of color reminiscent of Gaudi’s Casa Batlló.

The project was a month long, from creating the shirt and brand to the final lasercut packaging. I loved that this project got me up and out of the classroom to explore LA's fashion district for fabric and pushed me to wrangle handsewing a whole shirt.



As you may know by now, I'm Mimi, a designer & illustrator from sunny Bay Area currently based in even sunnier Los Angeles. I'm learning about product design and data visualization through a project based curriculum at the Iovine and Young Academy at USC.
